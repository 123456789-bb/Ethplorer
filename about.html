How it Works and Solves the Problem
Ethereum tokens are not built into its blockchain. Those are smart contracts that can be written as you wish. Therefore, except for a smart contract’s owner, it's really hard to detect the information inside such tokens.

There is no public independent third-party viewer, which creates a serious obstacle to popularizing Ethereum based tokens. Such a viewer could display the issuance history, description, and other token and blockchain attributes publicly available. Isn't it what we love the blockchain for?

Ethplorer is our big solution to this problem. It displays information about Ethereum tokens directly from its blockchain — not only for developers, but also for the public.

Recently, the EIP20 protocol was accepted by the Ethereum community and has become the first standard protocol for token creation and development (ERC20). However, many tokens, including the well-known DAO and Digix, were created before final version of EIP20. This means they were created with a few modifications that prevent reading them correctly.

We will be adding additional functionality to support these tokens’ display process. This includes adding ABI for contracts which creators would provide them to us.

So, if your token is shown incorrectly and you want fix that in Ethplorer, please let us know.

Considering the above, keep in mind that viewing tokens does not guarantee that all token information will be displayed correctly. This is a beta version of the viewer. Please leave your feedback and suggestions and we will keep developing Ethplorer to display the most useful and accurate information available.
